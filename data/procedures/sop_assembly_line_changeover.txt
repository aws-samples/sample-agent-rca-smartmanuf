1. PURPOSE
   This procedure defines the requirements for product changeover on shared
   assembly lines used for NEXUS immunoassay kit production. The procedure
   ensures complete removal of all components from the previous product
   before starting production of the next product, preventing component
   mix-up and cross-contamination.

2. SCOPE
   Applies to all product changeovers on assembly lines ASM-LINE-01 through
   ASM-LINE-03 in Building B, Room 201. Covers changeover between any two
   NEXUS products sharing assembly equipment.

3. REFERENCES
   - SOP-ASM-001: Assembly Line Operation
   - SOP-ASM-002: Component Loading and Verification
   - SOP-QC-040: In-Process Quality Checks
   - ISO 13485:2016 Section 7.5.1 (Control of production)
   - cGMP 21 CFR 820.70 (Production and process controls)

4. DEFINITIONS
   Line Clearance: Complete removal and verification of absence of all
     components, materials, and labeling from the previous product run.
   Changeover: The process of transitioning an assembly line from one
     product to another.
   First Article Inspection: Physical verification of the first assembled
     unit after changeover to confirm correct components.

5. CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS

   5.1 MINIMUM CHANGEOVER TIME: 30 MINUTES
       The changeover process requires a MINIMUM of 30 minutes to complete
       all clearance and verification steps. This time includes:
       - Physical clearance of line (10 min)
       - Cleaning of contact surfaces (5 min)
       - Loading of new components (5 min)
       - Verification and documentation (10 min)

       NOTE: Changeover time shall NOT be shortened for any reason including
       production schedule pressure. Any deviation from minimum changeover
       time must be documented as a non-conformity.

   5.2 LINE CLEARANCE CHECKLIST
       ALL 8 items must be verified and signed off before production restart.
       The checklist (form PR-CLC-001) includes:

       Item 1: Remove all previous product components from line
               - Empty all hoppers, feeders, and staging areas
               - Physically inspect all stations for residual components
       Item 2: Verify hopper emptied of previous SPR cones
               - SPR cone hopper must be completely empty
               - Visual confirmation required (open hopper lid, inspect)
               - Document number of residual cones removed (if any)
       Item 3: Clean all contact surfaces
               - Wipe down conveyor belts, guides, and fixtures
               - Use approved cleaning agent (IPA 70%)
       Item 4: Verify correct labeling materials loaded
               - Confirm label roll matches next product reference
               - Verify expiry date printing is correct
       Item 5: Scan component barcodes for next product
               - Scan each component type barcode against production order
               - System must confirm product reference match
               - CRITICAL: This step prevents wrong component loading
       Item 6: Verify correct reagent strips loaded
               - Confirm reagent strip lot matches production order
               - Visual check of strip color coding
       Item 7: First article inspection
               - Assemble and inspect first unit after changeover
               - Verify all components are correct for the intended product
               - Open kit and physically examine contents
       Item 8: Supervisor sign-off
               - Production supervisor must verify completed checklist
               - Supervisor must be physically present at the line
               - Supervisor signs and dates the clearance form

   5.3 LINE CLEARANCE VERIFICATION
       Before production of the next product can begin:
       a) All 8 checklist items must be completed and signed
       b) Supervisor sign-off obtained (Item 8)
       c) Clearance form filed in batch record
       d) Assembly line system unlocked by supervisor authorization

6. PROCEDURE

   6.1 Production Stop
       a) Complete current production run
       b) Record final unit count and lot number in batch record
       c) Stop assembly line and engage safety lockout

   6.2 Line Clearance (Minimum 15 minutes)
       a) Remove all components from hoppers, feeders, and stations
       b) Pay particular attention to SPR cone hoppers:
          - SPR cones for different products may be physically similar
          - Different products use different antibody coatings
          - Visual differentiation may be difficult without cap color check
       c) Collect and count all removed components
       d) Return unused components to warehouse with documentation
       e) Clean all contact surfaces per cleaning procedure

   6.3 Component Loading (Minimum 5 minutes)
       a) Retrieve components for next product from warehouse
       b) Verify component lot numbers against production order
       c) Load components into designated hoppers and feeders
       d) Scan component barcodes to verify correct product match

   6.4 Verification (Minimum 10 minutes)
       a) Complete all 8 items on Line Clearance Checklist
       b) Perform first article inspection
       c) Obtain supervisor sign-off
       d) File completed checklist in batch record

7. PRODUCT-SPECIFIC NOTES

   7.1 NEXUS CK MB (Reference 30421) to NEXUS B2M (Reference 30420)
       CAUTION: SPR cones for CK MB and B2M are physically similar in shape
       and size. Differentiation relies on:
       - Cap color: CK MB = no color; B2M = blue cap
       - Barcode: Different product reference encoded
       - Labeling: Product name printed on sachet
       Extra vigilance required during hopper clearance for this changeover
       combination.

   7.2 NEXUS Troponin (Reference 30422) to any product
       Troponin SPR cones have distinct red cap and larger diameter - lower
       mix-up risk.

8. NON-COMPLIANCE
   Any deviation from this procedure, including but not limited to:
   - Changeover time less than 30 minutes
   - Incomplete line clearance checklist
   - Missing supervisor sign-off
   - Skipped first article inspection
   shall be immediately documented as a non-conformity and reported to
   Quality Assurance.

9. RECORDS
   - Line Clearance Checklist (form PR-CLC-001)
   - Component Return Form (form PR-CRF-001)
   - First Article Inspection Report (form PR-FAI-001)
