ENTRY: FMEA-CKMB-001
PROCESS STEP: SPR Receipt and Inspection
POTENTIAL FAILURE MODE: SPR coating variation - antibody density outside
  nominal range but within specification
POTENTIAL EFFECT: Signal out of specification during calibration verification
  (S1 too high or too low)
SEVERITY (S): 8
  Justification: Product cannot be released; potential customer calibration
  failures if not caught
POTENTIAL CAUSE: Lot-to-lot variation in supplier coating process; antibody
  density at extreme ends of specification range
OCCURRENCE (O): 3
  Justification: Qualified supplier with established process; variation
  possible but infrequent between production batches
CURRENT CONTROLS: Incoming inspection per SOP-SPR-001 (antibody density
  measurement, COA review); specification limits 2.0-2.5 mg/mL
DETECTION (D): 4
  Justification: Incoming inspection measures antibody density but current
  specification allows wide range; borderline values at upper/lower spec
  may not trigger review unless exceeding limits
RPN: 96
ACTION RECOMMENDED: Consider tightening incoming specification or adding
  alert limits for values at extreme ends of specification range
ACTION OWNER: A. Laurent (QC Supervisor)
TARGET DATE: 2023-06-30

ENTRY: FMEA-CKMB-002
PROCESS STEP: Calibration Verification
POTENTIAL FAILURE MODE: Calibration drift - invalid calibration (error code B18)
POTENTIAL EFFECT: Product fails QC release testing; customer instruments
  unable to calibrate; potential for erroneous patient results if undetected
SEVERITY (S): 9
  Justification: Direct impact on diagnostic accuracy; patient safety risk
  if calibration failure not detected
POTENTIAL CAUSE: Equipment optical sensor degradation; SPR coating variation;
  calibrator degradation; environmental conditions
OCCURRENCE (O): 2
  Justification: Multiple potential causes but each individually rare;
  historical occurrence: 1 event in past 3 years (INV-2022-0045)
CURRENT CONTROLS: QC calibration verification per SOP-CAL-001; trend analysis
  system with automated alerts; quarterly PM on calibration stations
DETECTION (D): 5
  Justification: QC testing will detect failure at lot level; however, root
  cause identification requires investigation. Trend analysis may detect
  gradual drift before OOS.
RPN: 90
ACTION RECOMMENDED: Implement enhanced trend analysis with tighter alert
  limits; add correlation analysis between SPR lot and signal values
ACTION OWNER: M. Petit (QC Analyst)
TARGET DATE: 2023-03-31

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ENTRY: FMEA-CKMB-003

POTENTIAL FAILURE MODE: Reagent strip defect - incomplete reagent deposition
POTENTIAL EFFECT: Reduced assay sensitivity; potential false negative results
SEVERITY (S): 9
POTENTIAL CAUSE: Dispensing nozzle clog; reagent viscosity variation;

OCCURRENCE (O): 1

  QC functional testing
DETECTION (D): 3
RPN: 27
ACTION RECOMMENDED: None (low risk)

ENTRY: FMEA-CKMB-004
PROCESS STEP: Kit Assembly
POTENTIAL FAILURE MODE: Wrong component in kit - incorrect SPR cones or
  reagent strips from different product
POTENTIAL EFFECT: Completely invalid test results; wrong analyte measured;
  patient receives incorrect diagnosis
SEVERITY (S): 10
  Justification: Catastrophic - patient receives completely wrong test result
POTENTIAL CAUSE: Incomplete line clearance during product changeover;
  operator error in component loading; labeling error
OCCURRENCE (O): 2
  Justification: Changeover procedures and controls in place; historical
  near-miss (INV-2024-0067) but no customer-reaching events
CURRENT CONTROLS: Line clearance checklist (SOP-ASM-003); barcode scanning;
  in-process QC inspection; first article inspection
DETECTION (D): 3
  Justification: Multiple detection points (barcode scan, visual inspection,
  QC check) provide layered defense
RPN: 60
ACTION RECOMMENDED: Reinforce changeover procedure compliance; consider
  automated component verification at each assembly station
ACTION OWNER: P. Dubois (Production Manager)
TARGET DATE: 2023-09-30
