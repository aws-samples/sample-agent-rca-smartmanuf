PROCESS RISK ASSESSMENT: Assembly Line Changeover
DOCUMENT ID: PRA-ASM-001
REVISION: 2
EFFECTIVE DATE: 2025-01-15
PROCESS: NEXUS Kit Assembly - Product Changeover (ASM-LINE-01 to ASM-LINE-03)

ASSESSOR: M. Garnier (Production Manager)
REVIEWED BY: L. Fontaine (Quality Manager)

SCOPE:
This process risk assessment covers potential risks associated with product
changeover on shared NEXUS kit assembly lines. The assessment focuses on
component mix-up risks, cross-contamination, and procedural compliance
during line clearance.

REFERENCE PROCEDURES:
  - SOP-ASM-003 Rev 2: Assembly Line Product Changeover Procedure
  - SOP-ASM-001: Assembly Line Operation
  - SOP-QC-040: In-Process Quality Checks

================================================================================
RISK REGISTER
================================================================================

RISK: PR-001
TITLE: Incomplete line clearance leading to wrong component in kit
DESCRIPTION:
  During product changeover, components from the previous product are not
  completely removed from the assembly line. Residual components (SPR cones,
  reagent strips, or other kit components) from the previous product are
  inadvertently included in kits for the next product. This results in
  kits containing components coated with incorrect antibodies, leading to
  completely invalid test results for the end user.

POTENTIAL CAUSES:
  1. Operator fails to fully empty component hoppers during changeover
  2. Components lodged in hard-to-reach areas of feed mechanism
  3. Changeover performed too quickly (below minimum time requirement)
  4. Operator distraction or multitasking during clearance
  5. Supervisor sign-off without independent physical verification

AFFECTED PRODUCT COMBINATIONS (highest risk):
  - NEXUS CK MB (30421) <-> NEXUS B2M (30420): SPR cones physically similar,
    differentiation relies on cap color (CK MB: no color; B2M: blue cap)
  - NEXUS D-Dimer (30425) <-> NEXUS Troponin (30422): Reagent strips similar
    in size, differentiation by color coding

SEVERITY (S): 9
  Justification: Patient receives completely wrong test result; potential for
  misdiagnosis and inappropriate treatment. Class I recall may be required.
OCCURRENCE (O): 2
  Justification: Changeover procedures and multiple controls in place;
  one historical near-miss (INV-2024-0067, NEXUS Troponin) caught during
  in-process check; no customer-reaching events in past 5 years.
DETECTION (D): 3
  Justification: Multiple detection layers - barcode scanning (automated),
  in-process QC inspection (manual), first article inspection (manual).
  However, detection depends on all controls functioning and being executed.
RPN: 54

RISK LEVEL: MEDIUM (RPN 50-100)

EXISTING CONTROLS:
  1. SOP-ASM-003 Rev 2: 30-minute minimum changeover time
  2. 8-item line clearance checklist with supervisor sign-off
  3. Barcode scanning at component loading (automated detection)
  4. In-process QC inspection at Station 4
  5. First article inspection after changeover

CONTROL EFFECTIVENESS:
  Controls are effective WHEN FOLLOWED. Risk increases significantly if:
  - Changeover time is shortened (less time for thorough clearance)
  - Checklist items are marked without actual verification
  - Barcode scanner is bypassed or malfunctioning
  - Supervisor sign-off is performed remotely or without physical presence

ACTION RECOMMENDED: Reinforce procedural compliance through regular
  observation audits; consider automated line lockout if changeover time
  is below minimum
ACTION OWNER: M. Garnier (Production Manager)
TARGET DATE: 2025-06-30

RISK: PR-002
TITLE: Short changeover time leading to verification steps being skipped
DESCRIPTION:
  Production schedule pressure or other operational factors lead to
  changeover being performed in less than the SOP-required minimum of
  30 minutes. Shortened changeover time results in verification steps
  being rushed, abbreviated, or skipped entirely. This directly increases
  the probability of PR-001 (wrong component in kit) occurring.

POTENTIAL CAUSES:
  1. Production schedule pressure (rush orders, delivery deadlines)
  2. Understaffing on assembly line (fewer operators for changeover)
  3. Verbal authorization to shorten changeover by supervisor/management
  4. Lack of automated enforcement of minimum changeover time
  5. Cultural acceptance of procedural shortcuts under pressure

SEVERITY (S): 8
  Justification: Short changeover does not directly harm patients but
  significantly increases probability of component mix-up (PR-001);
  severity reflects the enabling effect on the higher-severity risk.
OCCURRENCE (O): 3
  Justification: Production schedule pressure is a recurring operational
  reality; while procedures prohibit shortening changeover, enforcement
  depends on human compliance; periodic audit findings suggest occasional
  marginal compliance with changeover timing.
DETECTION (D): 4
  Justification: Changeover time is recorded in machine stop logs but
  reviewed retrospectively (not real-time enforcement). Short changeovers
  may not be detected until post-production review or investigation.
RPN: 96

RISK LEVEL: MEDIUM (RPN 50-100)

EXISTING CONTROLS:
  1. SOP-ASM-003 specifies 30-minute minimum changeover time
  2. Machine stop logs record changeover duration
  3. Supervisor must authorize production restart
  4. Periodic process audits review changeover compliance

CONTROL GAP:
  No automated enforcement mechanism exists to prevent production restart
  if changeover time is below minimum. Current controls rely entirely on
  human compliance and retrospective review.

ACTION RECOMMENDED:
  1. Implement automated line lockout that prevents restart until minimum
     changeover time has elapsed
  2. Add real-time alert to production supervisor if changeover time
     approaches minimum
  3. Include changeover time compliance in operator performance metrics
ACTION OWNER: M. Garnier (Production Manager)
TARGET DATE: 2025-06-30

RISK: PR-003
TITLE: Barcode scanner failure during component loading verification
DESCRIPTION:
  Barcode scanner at component loading station fails or produces read errors,
  causing operators to bypass the automated component verification step. This
  removes a key automated detection control for wrong components.

POTENTIAL CAUSES:
  1. Scanner lens contamination (dust, residue)
  2. Scanner hardware failure
  3. Barcode label damage or poor print quality
  4. Scanner calibration drift

SEVERITY (S): 7
  Justification: Loss of one detection layer; other controls (visual
  inspection, QC check) remain active
OCCURRENCE (O): 2
  Justification: Scanner maintained monthly; historical failure rate low
DETECTION (D): 3
  Justification: Scanner errors are logged; operator override is recorded
  and flagged for review
RPN: 42

RISK LEVEL: LOW (RPN < 50)

EXISTING CONTROLS:
  - Monthly scanner calibration and cleaning (PM schedule)
  - Scanner override requires supervisor authorization
  - Override events logged and reviewed daily

ACTION RECOMMENDED: None (acceptable risk level with current controls)

incomplete clearance (PR-001) increases substantially. Current controls
depend heavily on human compliance with no automated enforcement.
