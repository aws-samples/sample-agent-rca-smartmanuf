HAZARD ASSESSMENT: NEXUS CK MB Calibration Process
DOCUMENT ID: HAZ-CAL-001
REVISION: 2
EFFECTIVE DATE: 2023-02-01
PRODUCT: NEXUS CK MB (Reference 30421)

ASSESSOR: J. Martin (Quality Manager)
REVIEWED BY: A. Laurent (QC Supervisor)

SCOPE:
This hazard assessment covers potential hazards associated with the NEXUS CK MB
calibration process, focusing on signal deviations that could impact diagnostic
accuracy and patient safety.

================================================================================
HAZARD REGISTER
================================================================================

HAZARD: HAZ-001
TITLE: S1 signal too high (above upper specification limit)
DESCRIPTION:
  S1 calibration signal exceeds 550 RFV upper specification limit. This
  results in error code B18 (invalid calibration) on NEXUS analyzers.
  If undetected and product released, customer instruments would either
  fail to calibrate (B18 error) or produce artificially elevated CK MB
  results leading to potential false positive cardiac marker diagnosis.

POTENTIAL CAUSES:
  1. SPR cone antibody density too high (e.g., supplier lot at upper end
     of specification or above specification)
  2. Calibrator S1 concentration above nominal
  3. Instrument optical sensor over-sensitivity
  4. Environmental temperature below range (increased fluorescence)

LIKELIHOOD: Unlikely (2/5)
CONSEQUENCE: Major (4/5) - Patient misdiagnosis risk
RISK LEVEL: MEDIUM (8/25)

EXISTING CONTROLS:
  - Incoming SPR inspection (antibody density measurement)
  - QC calibration verification per SOP-CAL-001
  - Automated B18 error detection on customer instruments
  - QC trend analysis with automated alerts

RESIDUAL RISK: Acceptable with current controls
MONITORING: Continuous QC trend analysis of S1 signal values

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

HAZARD: HAZ-002
TITLE: S1 signal too low (below lower specification limit)
DESCRIPTION:
  S1 calibration signal falls below 450 RFV lower specification limit. This
  results in error code B18 (invalid calibration) on NEXUS analyzers.
  If undetected and product released, customer instruments would either
  fail to calibrate or produce artificially low CK MB results leading to
  potential false negative cardiac marker diagnosis (missed cardiac event).

POTENTIAL CAUSES:
  1. SPR cone antibody density too low or coating degradation
  2. Calibrator S1 degradation (loss of potency)
  3. Instrument optical sensor degradation (reduced sensitivity)
  4. SPR cone storage conditions excursion (temperature, humidity)

LIKELIHOOD: Unlikely (2/5)
CONSEQUENCE: Critical (5/5) - Missed cardiac event, potential patient death
RISK LEVEL: HIGH (10/25)

EXISTING CONTROLS:
  - Incoming SPR inspection
  - QC calibration verification per SOP-CAL-001
  - Calibrator stability monitoring program
  - Equipment PM with optical sensor verification
  - QC trend analysis with automated alerts

ADDITIONAL CONTROLS IMPLEMENTED:
  - Lamp life monitoring added per INV-2022-0045 CAPA
  - Proactive lamp replacement at 1500 hours

RESIDUAL RISK: Acceptable with enhanced controls
MONITORING: Continuous QC trend analysis; quarterly equipment PM

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

HAZARD: HAZ-003
TITLE: S2 signal out of range
DESCRIPTION:
  S2 (blank/low calibrator) signal outside 80-120 RFV specification range.
  Results in error code B19. Indicates potential reagent strip issue or
  instrument background noise problem.

POTENTIAL CAUSES:
  1. Reagent strip contamination
  2. Instrument background noise
  3. Incorrect S2 calibrator lot

LIKELIHOOD: Remote (1/5)
CONSEQUENCE: Moderate (3/5)
RISK LEVEL: LOW (3/25)

EXISTING CONTROLS:
  - QC calibration verification
  - Reagent strip in-process testing
  - Automated B19 error detection

RESIDUAL RISK: Acceptable
MONITORING: Standard QC testing
