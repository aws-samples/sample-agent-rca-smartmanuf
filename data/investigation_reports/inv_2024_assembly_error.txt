INVESTIGATION REPORT: INV-2024-0067
================================================================================

TITLE: Assembly Line Component Mix-up - NEXUS Troponin
PRODUCT: NEXUS Troponin (Reference 30422)
DATE OPENED: 2024-09-10
DATE CLOSED: 2024-11-15
STATUS: Closed - CAPA Verified Effective

CLASSIFICATION: Process Deviation Investigation
PRIORITY: High
INVESTIGATION LEAD: S. Mitchell (Production Quality)
APPROVED BY: L. Fields (Quality Manager)

================================================================================
SECTION 1: PROBLEM STATEMENT
================================================================================

During in-process quality check on assembly line ASM-LINE-01, the QC inspector
identified incorrect reagent cards in a NEXUS Troponin kit being assembled.
NEXUS D-Dimer reagent cards (Reference 30425) were found loaded in the
reagent card feeder instead of NEXUS Troponin cards (Reference 30422). The
error was caught before any completed kits left the assembly area.

Affected lot: 1010500120 (NEXUS Troponin)
Previous product on line: NEXUS D-Dimer (lot 1010500119)
Detection point: In-process QC check (station 4 of 6)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: INVESTIGATION METHODOLOGY - QQOCQPC
================================================================================

QUI (Who):
  - Who detected the problem? QC Inspector R. Blake during routine in-process
    check
  - Who performed the changeover? Operator OP-022 (T. Mitchell)
  - Who supervised? SUP-005 (D. Brooks) - signed off changeover checklist

QUOI (What):
  - What is the problem? Wrong reagent cards loaded after product changeover
  - What product is affected? NEXUS Troponin (Reference 30422)
  - What component was wrong? Reagent cards from NEXUS D-Dimer (Reference
    30425) were in the feeder
  - What was correct? sensor cartridges were correct (Troponin cartridges have distinct
    red cap, easily identifiable)

OU (Where):
  - Where did the error occur? ASM-LINE-01, reagent card feeder (Station 3)
  - Where was it detected? Station 4 (QC in-process inspection point)
  - Where was the previous product run? Same line (ASM-LINE-01)

COMMENT (How):
  - How did the wrong cards get loaded? Incomplete clearance of reagent
    card feeder during changeover - D-Dimer cards remained in feeder
  - How was it detected? QC inspector visual check - D-Dimer cards have
    yellow color coding vs Troponin green color coding

QUAND (When):
  - When did the changeover occur? 2024-09-10, 10:15-10:50 (35 minutes)
  - When was the error detected? 2024-09-10, 11:30 (40 minutes into
    Troponin production)
  - When was production stopped? 2024-09-10, 11:32

POURQUOI (Why):
  - Why did the error occur? Reagent card feeder not fully cleared during
    changeover; operator focused on sensor cartridge changeover and overlooked
    reagent card verification
  - Why was it not caught earlier? Barcode scan at Station 3 was bypassed
    (scanner flagged "read error" and operator overrode)

COMBIEN (How much):
  - How many units affected? Approximately 150 partially assembled kits
    (all segregated and disassembled)
  - How many released to market? ZERO (caught during in-process check)
  - Financial impact: EUR 8,500 (material waste and production downtime)

================================================================================
SECTION 3: ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (5M / Ishikawa)
================================================================================

MATIERE (Materials):
  - D-Dimer and Troponin reagent cards are physically similar in size
  - Color coding present but subtle (yellow vs green)
  CONCLUSION: Material similarity is a CONTRIBUTING FACTOR but not root cause

METHODE (Method):
  - Changeover procedure SOP-ASM-003 Rev 2 was followed
  - Changeover time: 35 minutes (meets 30-minute minimum)
  - Checklist: 7 of 8 items completed
  - MISSING: Item 6 (Verify correct reagent cards loaded) was checked as
    completed but investigation found cards were not actually verified
  - Barcode scan (Item 5) returned "read error" and operator overrode
  CONCLUSION: Method compliance issue - CONFIRMED as root cause

MILIEU (Environment):
  - No environmental anomalies
  CONCLUSION: Environment ELIMINATED

MAIN D'OEUVRE (Personnel):
  - Operator OP-022: Experienced (3 years), training current
  - Operator reported being "in a hurry" to complete changeover
  - Supervisor SUP-005: Signed checklist without independently verifying
    all items
  CONCLUSION: Personnel compliance is a CONTRIBUTING FACTOR

MACHINE (Equipment):
  - Barcode scanner at Station 3: "Read error" logged at 10:48
  - Investigation found scanner lens was dirty (cleaned, function restored)
  - Scanner error contributed to failure to detect wrong cards
  CONCLUSION: Equipment is a CONTRIBUTING FACTOR

================================================================================
SECTION 4: ROOT CAUSE DETERMINATION
================================================================================

ROOT CAUSE: Incomplete changeover verification - reagent card feeder not
properly cleared and verified during product changeover. Operator marked
checklist Item 6 as complete without actual verification. Barcode scanner
error at Station 3 removed the automated detection control.

CATEGORY: Methode (Method) - Procedural non-compliance

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
1. Physical similarity of reagent cards between products
2. Barcode scanner malfunction (dirty lens)
3. Supervisor signed off without independent verification
4. Operator haste during changeover

================================================================================
SECTION 5: CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE:
1. All 150 partially assembled kits disassembled and components segregated
2. Line cleared and full changeover repeated
3. Troponin production restarted with enhanced supervision
4. Operator OP-022 retrained on changeover procedure

PREVENTIVE (CAPA-2024-0045):
1. Added barcode scanner maintenance to daily start-up checklist
2. Implemented mandatory scanner function verification before each changeover
3. Added dual-verification requirement for reagent card loading
   (operator + QC inspector)
4. Reinforced supervisor responsibility for independent checklist verification

EFFECTIVENESS VERIFICATION:
Monitoring period: October 2024 - January 2025 (4 months)
Result: No recurrence of component loading errors. All changeover checklists
verified complete with dual sign-off. Barcode scanner availability at 99.9%.
CAPA verified effective and closed.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: LESSONS LEARNED
================================================================================

1. In-process QC checks are an effective last line of defense for catching
   assembly errors. The QC inspection point at Station 4 successfully
   prevented affected product from being released.

2. Barcode scanning is a critical automated control for component
   verification. Scanner maintenance must be prioritized to ensure this
   control remains effective.

3. Changeover checklist completion requires not just marking items as done
   but actual physical verification. Training should emphasize the importance
   of each individual step.

4. Supervisor sign-off must involve independent verification, not just
   review of operator's marks.

NOTE: This investigation is relevant context for any future assembly line
mix-up investigations. The same assembly line (ASM-LINE-01) is used for
multiple NEXUS products and component mix-up risk exists whenever products
are changed over.
