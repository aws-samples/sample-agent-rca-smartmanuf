SECTION 1: PROBLEM STATEMENT
================================================================================

During routine QC trend analysis, a gradual downward drift in S1 calibration
signal was observed for NEXUS CK MB across multiple consecutive lots
manufactured between January and March 2022. S1 values decreased from a
nominal 500 RFV to approximately 460 RFV, approaching the lower specification
limit of 450 RFV. No lots failed specification but the trend was statistically
significant (p < 0.01).

Affected lots: 1009900820 through 1009900828 (9 lots)
Signal trend: S1 decreasing from ~500 RFV to ~460 RFV over 9 lots

================================================================================
SECTION 4: ROOT CAUSE DETERMINATION
================================================================================

ROOT CAUSE: Equipment aging - Xenon lamp in NEXUS Calibration Station 1
(EQ-CAL-001) approaching end of rated life, causing gradual decrease in
fluorescence detection sensitivity. This resulted in proportionally lower
S1 signal readings across consecutive lots.

CATEGORY: Machine (Equipment)

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
- PM interval (quarterly) did not include lamp life monitoring
- No predictive maintenance trigger for lamp replacement
- Gradual drift not immediately apparent in individual lot results

================================================================================
SECTION 5: CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE:
1. Replaced xenon lamp in EQ-CAL-001 (WO-2022-0095, 2022-03-20)
2. Retested affected lots on refurbished instrument
3. All 9 lots retested within specification - released for distribution

PREVENTIVE (CAPA-2022-0032):
1. Added lamp usage hours to PM checklist (trigger replacement at 1500 hours)
2. Implemented quarterly optical sensor performance trending
3. Added automated alert for S1 signal drift > 2 sigma from historical mean
4. Updated PM schedule to include lamp life monitoring

EFFECTIVENESS VERIFICATION:
Monitoring period: May 2022 - November 2022 (6 months)
Result: No recurrence of S1 drift. Lamp replaced proactively at 1500 hours
in August 2022. All lots within nominal S1 range (490-510 RFV).
CAPA verified effective and closed.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: CONCLUSION
================================================================================

The calibration drift was caused by equipment aging (xenon lamp degradation)
and not by material or process changes. This investigation established the
importance of predictive maintenance for optical components in NEXUS
calibration stations. The corrective actions implemented (lamp life monitoring
and proactive replacement) have been verified effective.

NOTE: This investigation is relevant as historical context for any future
calibration-related investigations on NEXUS CK MB. However, each new
investigation should independently evaluate all 5M categories, as the root
cause may differ.
